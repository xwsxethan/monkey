Power Pack

Power Pack is an ongoing research project of mine dealing with profiling the power consumption of mobile tablets. I'm working on this project under the direction of Dr. Kirk Cameron along with several members of his SCAPE lab.

Below you can find the abstract for a class project surrounding preliminary research with PowerPack as well as some related class papers, such as a mobile energy consumption survey and a mock proposal to the NSF.

Abstract

Mobile devices play an ever increasing role in today's always connected world, from handheld devices to the next potential super computer. However, numerous challenges plague developers wishing to capitalize on the mobile application (app) marketplace. Chiefly among these is how to effectively create an app that will generate sustained interest across a plurality of platforms. Software engineering has given us several development patterns that fall along a spectrum of tradeoffs, with ease of development and maintenance on one end and broadly defined user appeal on the other, such as writing applications utilizing PhoneGap or with the purely native Android platform. However, comparisons of these patterns chiefly examine performance (speed, latency) and aesthetics as critical measures that affect which alternative should be utilized in a given scenario. In this paper I will consider a tradeoff that has received less attention lately from the application development perspective: energy efficiency. By profiling energy consumption of these classes of apps at a fine-grained (component-by-component) level, future developers and researchers can better understand where energy is being lost and thus implement improvements.
